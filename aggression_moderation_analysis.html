Introduction

This report presents a moderation analysis examining whether unemotional traits moderate the relationship between hours spent gaming and aggression. Moderation analysis helps us understand when and for whom the effect of a predictor variable (gaming hours) on an outcome variable (aggression) is stronger or weaker based on the level of a third variable (unemotional traits).

The research question is: Does the relationship between gaming hours and aggression depend on an individual’s level of unemotional traits?

Centering Predictors

For moderation analysis, we center the predictor and moderator variables. Centering helps with interpretation by making the main effects represent the effect at the mean level of the other variable.

# Center the predictors
aggression$hours_centered <- scale(aggression$hours_spent_gaming, scale = FALSE)
aggression$unemotional_centered <- scale(aggression$unemotional_traits, scale = FALSE)

# Create the interaction term
aggression$interaction <- aggression$hours_centered * aggression$unemotional_centered

Moderation Analysis

# Define moderator levels
moderator_levels <- c(
  "Low (-1 SD)" = mean_unemotional - sd_unemotional,
  "Mean" = mean_unemotional,
  "High (+1 SD)" = mean_unemotional + sd_unemotional
)

# Create hours range
hours_range <- seq(min(aggression$hours_spent_gaming), 
                   max(aggression$hours_spent_gaming), 
                   length.out = 100)

# Calculate predictions for each moderator level
plot_data <- data.frame()
for(i in 1:length(moderator_levels)) {
  hours_centered <- hours_range - mean_hours
  unemotional_centered <- moderator_levels[i] - mean_unemotional
  interaction <- hours_centered * unemotional_centered
  
  predicted <- coefs[1] + 
               coefs[2] * hours_centered + 
               coefs[3] * unemotional_centered + 
               coefs[4] * interaction

    hours_spent_gaming = hours_range,
    predicted_aggression = predicted,
    moderator_level = names(moderator_levels)[i]
  )
  plot_data <- rbind(plot_data, temp)
}

Interpretation and Conclusions

Summary of Key Findings

This moderation analysis reveals that the relationship between gaming hours and aggression is critically dependent on an individual’s level of unemotional traits:

  1. Significant Moderation Effect: The interaction between gaming hours and unemotional traits is highly significant (p < .001), explaining 68.7% of the variance in aggression.

  2. Context-Dependent Effect: The effect of gaming on aggression is not uniform:

    • For individuals low in unemotional traits, gaming is associated with lower aggression
    • For individuals high in unemotional traits, gaming is associated with substantially higher aggression

  3. Practical Implications: This finding suggests that:

    • Gaming itself may not be universally problematic for aggression
    • Individual characteristics (specifically unemotional traits) determine whether gaming is associated with increased or decreased aggression
    • Interventions targeting gaming-related aggression should consider individual differences in emotional processing

Methodological Notes

  • Predictors were centered to aid interpretation
  • The interaction term captures how the effect of one variable changes as a function of another
  • Simple slopes analysis decomposes the interaction at meaningful levels of the moderator (-1 SD, mean, +1 SD)

Limitations

  • This analysis assumes linear relationships
  • Causal interpretation requires experimental or longitudinal data
  • Additional covariates may affect the relationship
